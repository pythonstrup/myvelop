## How I Felt at the Beginning

I loved Geultto Cohort 9.

I liked the person I was becoming: part of a community, connecting with others, writing, and growing. That was why I joined Cohort 10 as well.

As I began my final Geultto cohort, I set several goals.

1.  Enjoy it
2.  Get to know one person each month
3.  Submit each post one week early

They did not seem overly demanding.

Unlike during Geultto Cohort 9, I was no longer working overtime every day, so I thought I would have more room to enjoy the experience this time.

On top of that, I was participating with a coworker this time, which reduced the psychological burden.

'If my coworker joins a community activity, I can tag along\~'

'When I join other activities, I should invite people from work too\~'

I began with those positive thoughts.

But the thing I had worried about eventually happened.

"I already have so much on my plate. Will I really be able to participate properly in the Geultto community?"

That fleeting thought before I applied became reality.

![And that concern became reality...](../../../assets/blog/4/01.png)

*And that concern became reality...*

## I Wanted to Do Too Much

There was always plenty to do at work.

At the same time, I ran two study groups,

participated in open source contributions from July through December,

joined Toss Runner's High from December through January,

and worked as a PM at Nexters from January through February.

Despite my ambition that "This Geultto cohort will be different. I will participate more actively," the reality was that even handling my existing schedule was overwhelming.

From the second half of last year through the beginning of this year, I was in <strong>fever time</strong>.

![Consecutive combos trigger fever time (Source: https://biz.heraldcorp.com/article/10300285)](../../../assets/blog/4/02.jpg)

*Consecutive combos trigger fever time (Source: https://biz.heraldcorp.com/article/10300285)*

I poured nearly every moment available to me into development.

Development concepts that had been tangled in my head began fitting together like puzzle pieces, and my mind absorbed software knowledge like a sponge. It was a <strong>double growth-XP event</strong>. I did not want to miss that opportunity, so I challenged myself to take part in every activity I could.

Coincidentally,

'I will just apply and see. If I do not get in, so be it.'

I ended up being accepted into every activity I had applied to so casually.

I kept taking on more work while repeating, "I will focus on the Geultto community once this activity ends." In the end, Geultto naturally slipped down my priority list. I did not even manage a single coffee chat during this cohort.

As a result, my goals of "submitting posts one week early" and "getting to know one person each month" both ended in failure.

## I Had a Great Time

Still, I definitely achieved my first goal: "Enjoy it."

I was disappointed that I could not have any coffee chats, but I joined the backend roundtable and connected with other developers. I also gained a lot by sneaking occasional peeks at posts shared in our team channel and the curation channel.

I used two passes during the especially busy period of my Nexters work, but <strong>I never failed to submit a post.</strong> Even when I carved out spare moments to write, the work felt more like <strong>enjoyment</strong> than a burden. I loved thinking about what made a "good post" and carefully writing it one word at a time.

## Thank You to a Wonderful Community

(Assuming I finished writing the post,) is there another community where I could have an experience this good without paying a cent?

Geultto was the reason I could keep writing consistently despite being busy. Perhaps it was because I felt writing changing me. When I first joined Cohort 9, I had completely abandoned my blog. Now, when a good topic comes to mind, the blog is the first thing I open. (Of course, I also have plenty of half-written drafts piled up...)

Curation was particularly motivating. I loved being able to show fellow developers what I considered a "good post." Whenever one of my posts was selected, I felt incredibly proud.

![Selected for curation](../../../assets/blog/4/03.png)

*You cannot resist curation\~*

Geultto also gave me the opportunity to grow my career through Toss Runner's High.

![Geultto promotion for Toss Runner's High](../../../assets/blog/4/04.png)

While working as a PM at Nexters, I once posted a user-research survey in Geultto's open promotion channel. So many people participated that we received more than 100 responses in just two days, allowing us to move through planning quickly.

![User research](../../../assets/blog/4/05.png)

*User research*

Geultto was a community that changed me for the better, and I am profoundly grateful for it.

To the organizers, and to Seongyun Byun:

Thank you so much for everything.
